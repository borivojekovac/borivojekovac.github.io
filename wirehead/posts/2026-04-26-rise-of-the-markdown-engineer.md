# Rise of the Markdown Engineer
## The world has become too complex for ordinary mortals. Bugs piled up and became acceptable. The once-brilliant gears of the information highway rusted and cracked under the pressure of security patches. We kept specializing more and more narrowly, constrained by our limits, unable to cover the vastness of the technological landscape. But not anymore.

Machine room. Bright under cold light, droning, clattering, and beeping as always, but the pale man in his thirties, wearing a spotless white shirt and heavy, conspicuous frames, barely notices. The Shaolin-grade concentration he is known for has carried the programmer into a trance, deep into the zone, as he checks the stack of [punched cards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punched_card "Punched card on Wikipedia") one more time, clearly seeing in their perforated patterns the instructions and subroutines meant for the magnetic drum of the mighty [IBM 650](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IBM_650 "IBM 650 on Wikipedia"). Tens of thousands of digits impressed into the orientation of magnetic moments in the nickel-cobalt coating of a metal cylinder. One bad punch, he is sure, and a tenth of a millimeter of that drum ends up magnetized the wrong way round. The mainframe's cacophony seems to shout that every idle hour costs the company as much as his weekly pay. And [Fats Domino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fats_Domino "Fats Domino on Wikipedia") goes on at the [Neapolitan](https://www.setlist.fm/setlist/fats-domino/1956/neapolitan-city-showroom-new-york-ny-3c35963.html "More shows in November 1956 at Neapolitan City Showroom") in an hour. ["Blue Monday, how I hate blue Monday... got to work like a slave all day..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fFIFOak-76w "Blue Monday on YouTube") There goes his concentration, straight out the window. And it is not even Monday.

The student sat at the keyboard. It was an [aerodynamic typewriter bolted into a stand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teletype_Model_33 "Teletype Model 33 on Wikipedia"), more than thirty kilos of metal that looked built both for flight and for surviving direct fire from a 20mm anti-aircraft gun. But that was not what kept this shaggy, bloodshot swot pinned to his chair at half past midnight in the [terminal room](https://archive.dartmouthalumnimagazine.com/article/1982/1/1/the-conquest-of-kiewit-sort-of "Article on Kiewit Computing Center in Dartmouth Alumni Magazine") of [Dartmouth College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_College "Dartmouth College on Wikipedia")'s computing center. No, this keyboard was alive. It understood a language that was neither human nor machine. True, [BASIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dartmouth_BASIC "Dartmouth BASIC on Wikipedia") stretched the meaning of the word a little for the average Homo sapiens, but for someone who had already tried to express himself in assembly, this was poetry. Somewhere in the bowels of the campus, the great [GE-635](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GE-600_series "GE-600 Mainframe Series on Wikipedia") brain listened patiently and answered, through its copper nerves, a hundred such terminals at once. In the brief hush after he typed the final command of [The Sumerian Game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Sumerian_Game "The Sumerian Game on Wikipedia"), [The Moody Blues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Moody_Blues "The Moody Blues on Wikipedia") drifted from the little radio on the windowsill: ["Tuesday afternoon, I'm just beginning to see..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=b5N7qHmEgxA "Tuesday Afternoon on YouTube") But the rest of the words were swallowed by the clatter of the mechanism typing onto paper: "YOU ARE RULER OF LAGASH. POPULATION 95. LAND 1000 ACRES. GRAIN 2800 BUSHELS. HOW MUCH GRAIN FOR FOOD?" For the next six or seven minutes, he forgot to close his mouth.

Rewind. Stop. Play. ["Here she comes to say, good night, I'll get no sleep, tonight..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c5WZge6pHVU "Wednesday Week on YouTube") The analog magnetic pattern, amplified by two AA batteries inside a [Sony Walkman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walkman "Walkman on Wikipedia"), crackled through foam earpads for his ears alone. He felt for the switch on the back of the [DEC VT100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/VT100 "DEC VT100 on Wikipedia") terminal, and a few moments and one ritual exchange of credentials later, [BSD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berkeley_Software_Distribution "BSD on Wikipedia") anticlimactically printed "%" in the cold glow of [P4 phosphor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_phosphorus "White Phosphorus on Wikipedia") excited by an electron gun. "[vi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vi_(text_editor) "vi text editor on Wikipedia") queue.c", RETURN. He was proud of his gift for writing portable [C code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C_(programming_language) "C programming language on Wikipedia"), clear enough that even someone without a doctorate could read it without strain. Grouping messages and stripping out redundancy would significantly cut the time modems needed to exchange mail between his [Berkeley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley "University of California, Berkeley on Wikipedia") and her [University of Sydney](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Sydney "University of Sydney on Wikipedia"). More importantly, it would increase the number of messages they would be able to exchange from now on. [The Undertones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Undertones "The Undertones on Wikipedia") kept repeating "Wednesday week, she loved me, Wednesday week, never happened at all..." for the hundredth time that night. Flawlessly coded. And now the quick combo of a digital ninja in a technicolor turtleneck: [make](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Make_(software) "Make on Wikipedia"), [diff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diff "Diff on Wikipedia"), [mail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mail_(Unix) "Mail on Wikipedia") - "Build passes on our VAX. If it fails on yours, I'm blaming Sydney, not the patch." What do the Undertones know anyway. Besides, Australia is not that far. He watched the sun come up, oblivious to the birds and the synthetic sound in his ears.

In a rented office in [Palo Alto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California "Palo Alto on Wikipedia"), decorated with discarded cardboard coffee cups and feral cables, a [15-inch JVC](https://crtdatabase.com/crts/jvc/jvc-tm-h150cg "JVC TM H150CG on CRT Database") sat on a metal shelf in the corner, showing an [MTV](https://sr.wikipedia.org/wiki/MTV "MTV on Wikipedia") broadcast of a [Tanita Tikaram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanita_Tikaram "Tanita Tikaram on Wikipedia") concert with almost no static at all. Okay, I have a window. And I have a document. Does the document have a window? Or is the window its child? Strange thoughts swarmed beneath the bleached hair of the twenty-five-year-old as he stared at colorful [Emacs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emacs "Emacs on Wikipedia") on his [Sun SPARCstation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SPARCstation "SPARCstation on Wikipedia") and clicked the mouse. Still, when the pieces finally snapped into place, his [C++ code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%2B%2B "C++ on Wikipedia") was immaculate: the outward form of an orderly chain of thought, easy to extend and easy to understand, the kind of thing that could open the door to explosive growth for their startup. His mind, until then submerged in the intricate abstractions, surfaced just in time for the chorus: ["But somewhere in the scheme of things, We'll find who's Thursday's child..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3RRCXqO8i9M "Thursday's Child on YouTube") and he smiled at the monitor.

The new Always-On [ADSL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ADSL "ADSL on Wikipedia") was a blessing to many Dutch people. For him, it was a curse. The people who designed the labyrinthine billing logic for the existing services had never imagined anyone might stay online continuously. Plenty of engineers would have slapped another assortment of hideous if-then-else blocks onto the already painful knot of existing ones and kicked the problem onward to some anonymous colleague in the future. But his [OCD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsessive%E2%80%93compulsive_disorder "OCD on Wikipedia") would not allow it. Sure, some people would have finished this by Monday and would not have let Friday evening arrive with the [vrijdagmiddagborrel](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/vrijdagmiddagborrel "Vrijdagmiddagborrel on Wiktionary") still waiting. Not him. He slowly rolled the wheel on his [iPod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPod "iPod on Wikipedia") until the familiar bars began. He glanced at the [GoF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Design_Patterns "Design Patterns on Wikipedia") on the shelf, dropped his gaze to [JBuilder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JBuilder "JBuilder on Wikipedia"), and once more reviewed DialUpBillingStrategy, AlwaysOnAdslStrategy, BusinessAdslStrategy... Beautiful. Subscriber with its calculate method. He reached for F9, then turned to the other monitor. The profiler showed the heap swelling with subscribers, plans, sessions, and discounts breeding organically out of the database, then deflating again in great breaths of [GC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garbage_collection_(computer_science) "Garbage collection on Wikipedia"). Some time later, after whole families and generations of virtual entities had lived out their turbulent lives assembling the monthly bill for the [ISP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_service_provider "Internet service provider on Wikipedia") he worked for, they left their creator a final message: "ok". [Robert Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Smith_(musician) "Robert Smith on Wikipedia") seemed to be reading his mind: ["...You can never get enough, Enough of this stuff, It's Friday, I'm in love..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mGgMZpGYiy8 "Friday I'm in Love on YouTube")

All the "real" programmers told her that [JavaScript](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JavaScript "JavaScript on Wikipedia") was shit. But it had something no other language did: the Internet. Release after release, layer after layer, it worked itself deep into humanity's nervous system, quietly and stubbornly, until the two had fused. Perpetually underestimated, it was becoming harder and harder to sneer at. One corner of her mouth twitched into a smile as she looked at the 14-inch LCD on her [X1 Carbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ThinkPad_X1_series "ThinkPad X1 Carbon series on Wikipedia"). She found "<input [v-model](https://vuejs.org/guide/components/v-model "Vue.js component v-model on vuejs.org")='user.email' />" and rebound it to "account.primaryEmail". That was enough. She did not write how the value moved, how it was validated, or how it got sent to the server. She just changed the truth. The framework knew what to do. The fan began pushing air over the [i7](https://hr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intel_Core_i7 "Intel Core i7 on Wikipedia") and its 16GB of [LPDDR3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LPDDR "LPDDR on Wikipedia") as the machine went into overdrive. "[Ok Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Assistant "Google Assistant on Wikipedia"), play [Spotify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spotify "Spotify on Wikipedia")," she told her [Pixel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Pixel "Google Pixel on Wikipedia"). Save. [Commit](https://git-scm.com/docs/git-commit "git commit on git-scm.com"). [Push](https://git-scm.com/docs/git-push "git push on git-scm.com"). [CI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continuous_integration "Continuous integration on Wikipedia") picked up the change. The build agent woke. The tests went green. Piano in the headphones. A new layer of bits was smeared into the [Docker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Docker_(software) "Docker on Wikipedia") image. A fresh version materialized in the registry. It started flowing down the pipeline. Horns. One new application instance in Europe. Then another. Then ten. [Chicago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_(band) "Chicago on Wikipedia") chimed in: ["Saturday in the park, I think it was the Fourth of July..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HjylD7esXDo "Saturday in the Park on YouTube") The old instances began to cool and shut down, and behind them the new ones started flashing in fireworks choreography, continent after continent. "People dancing, people laughing..." The Internet had just shed a little skin. She closed the laptop and slid it back into her backpack. She got up from the curb and looked for her crew. They were still waiting in line outside [Berghain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berghain "Berghain on Wikipedia"). She lowered her headphones and stepped into the heavy industrial rhythm and body heat just as a fine rain began to fall from the Berlin evening sky.

Dawn again. Let's just finish this one feature and then I will sleep. It is the weekend, sure, but tomorrow is a workday, you need a bit of rest. I look at my "code": eight [Markdown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Markdown "Markdown on Wikipedia") files that clearly describe the functionality I want. Dense technical English. A series of compromises carving, out of the universe of possibilities, a detailed specification of the thing I actually want. [BM25](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Okapi_BM25 "Okapi BM25 on Wikipedia"), combined with the existing [vector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vector_database "Vector database on Wikipedia") [search](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cosine_similarity "Cosine similarity on Wikipedia"), will form a hybrid algorithm for ranking the knowledge available to my election-monitoring assistant. It should restore, perhaps even improve, the accuracy of its [RAG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retrieval-augmented_generation "Retrieval-augmented generation on Wikipedia") after doubling the knowledge I put at its disposal. I type the final instruction: "...use incremental delivery implementation strategy please." While [Opus 4.6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claude_(language_model) "Claude language model on Wikipedia"), following [Spec Kit](https://github.com/github/spec-kit "Spec Kit on GitHub") instructions, thinks out loud and checks task after task of tasks.md with the inevitability of a slow tsunami, another artificial intelligence selects the next song. Over the first delicate notes of a musical box, [Lou Reed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lou_Reed "Lou Reed on Wikipedia") whispers ["Sunday morning brings the dawn in..."](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Xhbyj8pqUao "Sunday Morning on YouTube")

In a moment of lucidity, the history of programming flashed before my eyes in the death rattle of one industry and the rebirth of another. The world has become too complex for ordinary mortals. Bugs piled up and became acceptable. The once-brilliant gears of the information highway rusted and cracked under the pressure of security patches. We kept specializing more and more narrowly, constrained by our limits, unable to cover the vastness of the technological landscape. But not anymore. On top of layer after layer of old chrome tools, we have added a new one. Digital flesh. Soft and not entirely reliable, yet terrifyingly resourceful, agents are swarming through the non-space of the Internet, not entirely under control. "Watch out, the world's behind you". Just as with [penicillin a hundred years earlier](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/health/the-real-story-behind-the-worlds-first-antibiotic "The story of the discovery of penicillin on PBS"), the answer is sitting right in front of us. Not the product of some strategy drawn up by an army of the greatest individuals alive, but the product of the endless evolutionary dance of people, companies, ideas, and tools. Just as it once underestimated JavaScript, humanity has underestimated Markdown. A new caste is rising. The riders of agents are coming, and they speak eloquent neo-English Markdown. "Sunday morning." And before long, the next week begins.
